Medical emergencies don't follow business hours. Whether you need urgent medication at midnight, prescription refills on weekends, or emergency supplies during holidays, knowing where to find a 24 hour pharmacy in Kathmandu can be life-saving. This comprehensive guide covers night pharmacy Nepal options, major pharmacies open late in Kathmandu, hospital-based chemists, medicine delivery services, and tips for accessing emergency medication across Nepal.

🚨 Emergency Medicine Tip

If you cannot find an open pharmacy immediately, call your nearest hospital's emergency department. Hospital pharmacies at major medical centers operate 24/7 and can dispense emergency medication even if you are not admitted.

Major Pharmacy Chains in Nepal

Several pharmacy chains operate across Kathmandu and major Nepali cities. While not all individual outlets are open 24 hours, their larger branches and selected locations maintain extended or round-the-clock operations.

Metro Pharmacy

Metro Pharmacy is one of the largest pharmacy chains in Nepal with multiple outlets in Kathmandu. Their central branches in areas like New Road, Durbar Marg, and Maharajgunj offer extended hours and home delivery services.

  • Head Office: New Road, Kathmandu
  • Phone: 01-4412000
  • Delivery: Same-day delivery available within Kathmandu Ring Road
  • Hours: Most outlets open 7:00 AM – 9:00 PM; select branches 24 hours

DL Pharma

DL Pharma operates a network of modern pharmacies with computerized inventory and online ordering capabilities. Their main branches stock imported medicines, generic alternatives, and wellness products.

  • Main Branch: Durbar Marg, Kathmandu
  • Phone: 01-4444444
  • Online: Phone and Viber orders accepted for delivery

Medico Pharmacy

Medico Pharmacy has established itself as a trusted name for both allopathic and homeopathic medicines in Kathmandu. They maintain competitive pricing and stock a broad range of chronic disease medications.

  • Main Branch: Putalisadak, Kathmandu
  • Phone: 01-4240000
  • Specialty: Diabetes, blood pressure, and cardiac medications

Medicine Delivery Services in Nepal

For those unable to visit a pharmacy in person, several medicine delivery services operate in Kathmandu and expanding to other cities. These services are particularly valuable for elderly patients, people with mobility challenges, and during late-night emergencies.

Online Medicine Delivery Platforms

  • 1mg Nepal: Online ordering with delivery across Kathmandu Valley. Browse medicines, upload prescriptions, and receive home delivery within 2–4 hours.
  • Hamro Pharma: Local Nepali startup offering medicine delivery via mobile app. Covers Kathmandu, Lalitpur, and Bhaktapur with plans to expand.
  • Pathao & Tootle Delivery: While primarily ride-sharing apps, riders can be requested for urgent medicine pickup and delivery from nearby pharmacies.
  • Pharmacy Direct Call: Many pharmacies including Metro and DL accept phone orders with cash-on-delivery or online payment options.

📱 How to Order Medicine Online in Nepal

  1. Download the pharmacy app or visit their website
  2. Search for your medicine or upload a photo of your prescription
  3. Add items to cart and provide delivery address
  4. A pharmacist will verify your prescription (required for Schedule H drugs)
  5. Pay online or opt for cash on delivery
  6. Receive medicines at your doorstep within 2–6 hours

What to Know Before Buying Medicine in Nepal

  1. Prescription Requirements: Antibiotics, sleeping pills, and strong painkillers require a doctor's prescription. Always carry one for these medications.
  2. Generic Options: Nepali pharmacies stock both branded and generic medicines. Generic versions are significantly cheaper and equally effective.
  3. Price Variation: Medicine prices can vary between pharmacies. Government-regulated price lists are available at doph.gov.np.
  4. Storage Conditions: Insulin, vaccines, and some antibiotics require refrigeration. Verify cold chain storage at the pharmacy before purchasing.
  5. Expiry Dates: Always check manufacturing and expiry dates, especially when buying from smaller shops.
