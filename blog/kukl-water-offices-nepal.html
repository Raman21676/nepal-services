What is KUKL?

Kathmandu Upatyaka Khanepani Limited (KUKL) is a government-owned water supply and sanitation company that serves the Kathmandu Valley, including Kathmandu, Lalitpur, and Bhaktapur districts. Established to modernize water distribution in Nepal's capital region, KUKL manages the Melamchi Water Supply Project and other sources to deliver drinking water to millions of residents.

Despite significant infrastructure investments, many households in Kathmandu still face irregular water supply, low pressure, and contamination issues. Understanding how to contact KUKL and use their services can help you resolve problems faster and stay informed about your water rights.

📞 KUKL Main Contact Information

KUKL Headquarters

  • Address: Tripureshwor, Kathmandu, Nepal
  • Phone: 01-5970236, 01-5970237
  • Toll-Free Complaint: 198
  • Website: www.kukl.gov.np
  • Email: info@kukl.gov.np

The KUKL complaint number 198 is a toll-free helpline available for all water supply-related complaints in the Kathmandu Valley. You can call this number to report issues such as pipe leaks, no water supply, contaminated water, billing disputes, and meter problems. The line is operational during business hours, and urgent complaints are escalated to the relevant branch office.

📝 How to Register a Water Complaint with KUKL

If you're experiencing water supply issues, KUKL provides multiple channels to register complaints. Follow these steps for the fastest resolution:

  1. Call the KUKL Complaint Number: Dial 198 (toll-free) and explain your issue to the operator. Provide your customer ID, address, and a clear description of the problem.
  2. Visit Your Local Branch: For complex issues like meter replacement, pipeline damage, or billing disputes, visiting the branch with supporting documents is more effective.
  3. Use the KUKL Online Portal: The KUKL website offers an online complaint registration system where you can track the status of your complaint.
  4. Email Your Complaint: Send detailed complaints with photos (if applicable) to info@kukl.gov.np for documentation.
  5. Contact Your Ward Office: If KUKL does not respond within a reasonable timeframe, escalate the matter through your local municipal ward office.

🚛 KUKL Water Tanker Services

During dry seasons, maintenance shutdowns, or supply disruptions, KUKL provides emergency water tanker services to affected areas. These tankers deliver water directly to neighborhoods or individual households that have pre-registered for the service.

How to Request a KUKL Water Tanker

  • Call the KUKL emergency line or your local branch during supply disruptions.
  • Register through the KUKL mobile app if available in your area.
  • Contact your ward representative to arrange community tanker delivery.
  • Keep your customer ID ready when requesting tanker service.

In addition to KUKL tankers, many private water suppliers operate in Kathmandu. While convenient, private tanker water quality may not be guaranteed, so always verify the source before using it for drinking.

💰 KUKL Water Bill Payment Options

KUKL offers several convenient methods to pay your water bill:

  • Online Payment: Pay through the KUKL website using eSewa, Khalti, or connectIPS.
  • Mobile Wallets: Use eSewa, Khalti, or IME Pay apps to pay bills using your customer ID.
  • Bank Deposit: Deposit payments at designated partner banks including Nepal Investment Bank, Himalayan Bank, and others.
  • Branch Counter: Visit any KUKL branch office and pay directly at the billing counter.
  • Bank Counter: Some banks accept KUKL bill payments over the counter.

Late payments may incur penalties, so it's advisable to pay your water bill before the due date printed on your monthly statement. If you haven't received a bill, contact KUKL to verify your customer ID and mailing address.

💡 Tips for Water Supply Issues in Nepal

  • Always store at least 2-3 days of water for emergencies.
  • Install a water filter or purification system, as tap water may not be safe to drink directly.
  • Regularly check for leaks in your household plumbing to avoid inflated bills.
  • Keep your KUKL customer ID and latest bill handy when calling the complaint number.
  • Report illegal water connections in your area to help improve overall supply pressure.
