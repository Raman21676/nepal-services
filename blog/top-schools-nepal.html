School Admission Tips for Parents in Nepal

Securing a spot at the best schools in Kathmandu requires preparation. Follow these proven tips:

  1. Start Early: Research schools a year in advance. Visit campuses during open days.
  2. Understand the Curriculum: Choose between Nepal National Curriculum (SEE + NEB), CBSE, Cambridge (IGCSE/A-Level), or IB based on your child's future goals.
  3. Prepare for Entrance Exams: Most top private schools conduct entrance tests. Practice English, Mathematics, and General Knowledge with your child.
  4. Prepare Documents: Keep birth certificate, transfer certificate, previous report cards, passport photos, and character certificate ready.
  5. Apply to Multiple Schools: Don't rely on one institution. Apply to 3-5 schools to increase admission chances.
  6. Consider Location: Kathmandu traffic can add 1-2 hours to a commute. Choose a school within reasonable distance.

Curriculum Options in Nepali Schools

Nepali schools offer several curriculum choices. Understanding these helps parents align education with their child's university aspirations:

  • National Curriculum (SEE + NEB): The standard government curriculum. SEE at Grade 10, then +2 (NEB) in Science, Management, Humanities, or Education streams.
  • CBSE (Central Board of Secondary Education): Indian curriculum popular in private schools. Recognized across South Asia and globally.
  • Cambridge International (IGCSE & A-Levels): British curriculum offering internationally recognized qualifications. Excellent for students planning to study abroad.
  • International Baccalaureate (IB): Offered by select schools like Premier International. Globally respected and accepted by top universities worldwide.
  • American Curriculum (Advanced Placement): Followed by Lincoln School. Uses GPA system and AP courses for university credit.

Frequently Asked Questions About Schools in Nepal

Which is the best school in Nepal?

Budhanilkantha School is widely considered the top government school with outstanding academic results and alumni. Among private institutions, Rato Bangala School, St. Xavier's, Lincoln School, and KISC consistently rank at the top for their academic rigor, facilities, and university placement records.

What are the best private schools in Kathmandu?

The best private schools in Kathmandu include Rato Bangala School, St. Mary's School, Little Angels' School, GEMS School, Kathmandu International Study Centre (KISC), Premier International IB World School, and Lincoln School. These offer excellent facilities, experienced faculty, and strong extracurricular programs.

How much do private schools cost in Nepal?

Private school fees vary significantly. Local private schools charge approximately Rs. 20,000-80,000 per year. Mid-tier institutions cost Rs. 100,000-300,000 annually. Premium international schools like Lincoln School and KISC charge between Rs. 500,000-1,500,000+ per year including admission, facility, and activity fees.

When does school admission start in Nepal?

School admissions in Nepal typically open in February-March for the academic year beginning in mid-April (Baisakh). Entrance exams for competitive schools are usually held in January-February. Parents should apply early as popular schools fill seats quickly.

What curriculum do schools in Nepal follow?

Most Nepali schools follow the National Curriculum (SEE + NEB). Private schools may offer CBSE (Indian), Cambridge International (IGCSE/A-Levels), or International Baccalaureate (IB) programs. Choose based on your child's future education goals and budget.
