Nepal is one of the world's most welcoming destinations for travelers, offering Himalayan treks, cultural heritage sites, and spiritual retreats. However, even in paradise, emergencies can happen. Whether you're dealing with altitude sickness on the Everest Base Camp trek, a lost passport in Kathmandu, or a medical issue in Pokhara, having the right tourist emergency Nepal contacts at your fingertips is essential. This guide covers the tourist helpline Nepal, tourist police Nepal 1144, embassy contacts, trekking rescue numbers, and everything else a visitor needs to stay safe.

Tourist Police in Nepal

The Tourist Police Nepal is a specialized unit dedicated to assisting foreign visitors. Officers are trained to communicate in English and handle issues unique to tourists including theft, scams, lost documents, harassment, and safety concerns. The tourist police Nepal 1144 helpline is available round-the-clock from anywhere in Nepal.

Tourist police booths and offices are strategically located in areas with high visitor traffic:

  • Kathmandu: Thamel (main tourist police office), Basantapur Durbar Square, Pashupatinath
  • Pokhara: Lakeside tourist police office
  • Chitwan: Sauraha tourist police post
  • Lumbini: Lumbini Development Zone tourist police
  • Nagarkot: Tourist police checkpoint

What Tourist Police Can Help With

  • Filing police reports for theft or lost items
  • Assisting with lost or stolen passports
  • Addressing tourist scams and overcharging
  • Providing safety advice for trekking and travel
  • Coordinating with embassies and local authorities
  • General guidance and translation assistance

Trekking Emergency Contacts & Rescue

Nepal's trekking routes are remote, and emergencies like altitude sickness (AMS), injuries, and sudden weather changes require immediate action. Always purchase travel insurance that covers helicopter evacuation above 4,000 meters before starting any trek.

Himalayan Rescue Association (HRA)

The HRA operates aid posts in key trekking regions and provides medical assistance, altitude sickness treatment, and evacuation coordination.

  • Kathmandu Office: 01-4440292 / 01-4440293
  • Pokhara Office: 061-464098
  • Manang Aid Post: Operates during trekking seasons (Oct-Nov, Mar-Apr)
  • Pheriche Aid Post: Everest region medical support

Helicopter Rescue Companies

These companies provide emergency helicopter evacuation from trekking areas. Your insurance company or trekking agency typically arranges these, but direct contact may be needed in extreme emergencies:

  • Simrik Air: 01-4436244
  • Fishtail Air: 01-4436245
  • Altitude Air: 01-4436900
  • Shree Airlines: 01-4436272

Altitude Sickness Warning Signs

If you or a fellow trekker experience these symptoms, descend immediately and seek medical help:

  • Severe headache not relieved by painkillers
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Dizziness and loss of coordination
  • Shortness of breath at rest
  • Confusion or altered mental state
  • Coughing with frothy or pink sputum (HAPE sign)

Essential Safety Tips for Tourists in Nepal

  1. Register with Your Embassy: Many embassies offer online registration for travelers. This helps them locate you during emergencies.
  2. Buy Comprehensive Travel Insurance: Ensure coverage includes trekking above 4,000m, helicopter evacuation, and medical repatriation.
  3. Share Your Itinerary: Leave a copy of your trekking route and expected return dates with someone in Kathmandu.
  4. Carry Emergency Cash: Remote areas may not have ATMs. Carry enough rupees for unexpected transport or medical costs.
  5. Save Offline Maps: Download offline maps of your trekking route as mobile signal is unreliable in mountains.
  6. Avoid Solo Trekking: Trek with a partner or group. Solo trekkers in remote areas face higher risks.
  7. Respect Local Customs: Dress modestly at religious sites. Ask permission before photographing people.
  8. Drink Safe Water: Use purified or bottled water. Waterborne illnesses are common among travelers.
