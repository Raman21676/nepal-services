Every individual deserves to live with dignity, safety, and respect. In Nepal, several dedicated helplines and support organizations work tirelessly to protect children and women from abuse, violence, and exploitation. Whether you need immediate assistance, counseling, legal guidance, or simply someone to talk to, this comprehensive guide covers the child helpline Nepal (1098), women helpline Nepal (1145), domestic violence Nepal helpline resources, and mental health support services available across the country.

👶 CHILD HELPLINE NEPAL

1098

Toll-Free • Confidential • 24/7 • Nepali & English

Child Helpline 1098 Nepal

The child helpline Nepal 1098 is a national toll-free service dedicated to the protection and welfare of children. Operated by organizations like Child Helpline Nepal (CHN) and CWIN Nepal (Child Workers in Nepal Concerned Centre), this helpline provides a safe space for children and concerned adults to report abuse, seek guidance, and access support services.

Services Provided by Child Helpline 1098

  • Reporting child abuse, neglect, and exploitation
  • Crisis counseling for children in distress
  • Missing child reports and search coordination
  • Child labor complaints and rescue coordination
  • Referrals to child protection services and shelters
  • Psychological support for traumatized children
  • Legal information regarding child rights
  • Guidance for parents and guardians

When you call 1098, trained child protection counselors answer your call. They assess the situation, provide immediate emotional support, and connect callers with local child welfare committees, district child rights officers, or police as needed. All calls are treated confidentially, and callers can choose to remain anonymous.

👩 WOMEN HELPLINE NEPAL

1145

Toll-Free • Confidential • Crisis Support • 24/7

Women Helpline 1145 Nepal

The women helpline Nepal 1145 is the national emergency support line for women experiencing violence, harassment, discrimination, or any form of gender-based abuse. Operated by the National Women Commission (NWC) of Nepal in collaboration with various women's rights organizations, this helpline provides immediate crisis intervention, safety planning, and referral services.

Trained female counselors staff the helpline, ensuring that callers feel understood and supported. The service handles cases of domestic violence, sexual assault, workplace harassment, trafficking, forced marriage, witchcraft allegations, and dowry-related abuse.

What Happens When You Call 1145

  1. A trained counselor answers your call confidentially
  2. You describe your situation at your own pace
  3. The counselor assesses immediate safety risks
  4. Crisis intervention and emotional support are provided
  5. You receive information about your legal rights and options
  6. Referrals are made to safe houses, legal aid, or medical services if needed
  7. Follow-up calls can be arranged for ongoing support

Domestic Violence Support in Nepal

Domestic violence remains one of the most underreported crimes in Nepal due to social stigma, economic dependence, and lack of awareness about available support. The domestic violence Nepal helpline network includes government services, NGOs, and community-based organizations working together to protect survivors.

Recognizing Domestic Violence

Domestic violence includes physical abuse, emotional and psychological manipulation, sexual coercion, financial control, and social isolation. If you or someone you know experiences any of the following, help is available:

  • Hitting, kicking, slapping, or any physical harm
  • Threats of violence against you, children, or family members
  • Controlling behavior regarding money, movement, or communication
  • Forced sexual acts or marital rape
  • Verbal abuse, humiliation, or constant criticism
  • Preventing you from seeing friends, family, or working
  • Destruction of personal property or pets

Safe Houses and Shelters

For women and children who need to leave an unsafe home immediately, several shelters operate in Kathmandu and other cities:

  • Maiti Nepal Transit Home: Kathmandu – temporary shelter for trafficking survivors and domestic violence victims
  • SAATHI Crisis Center: Kathmandu – emergency accommodation and counseling
  • WOREC Safe Homes: Kathmandu and regional offices – comprehensive rehabilitation support
  • Local Women's Development Offices: Every district has government-run support services

Legal Rights and Protections in Nepal

Nepal has enacted several laws to protect children and women. Knowing your rights empowers you to seek justice:

  • Domestic Violence and Punishment Act, 2066 (2009): Criminalizes physical, mental, sexual, and economic abuse within domestic relationships. Allows for protection orders.
  • Children's Act, 2075 (2018): Guarantees children's rights to protection, education, and participation. Criminalizes child labor, trafficking, and abuse.
  • Human Trafficking and Transportation (Control) Act, 2064 (2007): Provides strict penalties for trafficking and comprehensive protection for survivors.
  • Muluki Criminal Code, 2074: Contains provisions against sexual violence, rape, and harassment with victim protection measures.
  • National Women Commission Act: Empowers the NWC to investigate complaints and recommend action on women's rights violations.

How to Help Someone in Need

If you suspect a child or woman is experiencing abuse, you can make a difference:

  1. Listen Without Judgment: Believe them. Many survivors fear not being believed.
  2. Provide Information: Share helpline numbers 1098 and 1145. Offer to help them make the call.
  3. Respect Their Choices: Leaving an abusive situation is complex. Support their decisions without pressure.
  4. Report if Necessary: If a child is in immediate danger, call 1098 or contact local police (100).
  5. Maintain Confidentiality: Never share their story without explicit permission.

Remember: You Are Not Alone

Help is available. Whether you are a child facing bullying, a woman experiencing violence, or someone struggling with mental health, trained professionals are ready to listen and support you. Calling 1098 or 1145 is the first step toward safety and healing. These calls are free, confidential, and could save a life.
